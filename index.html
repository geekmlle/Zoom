What is Google Glass?

Google Glass is a type of wearable technology with an optical head-mounted display (OHMD). It was developed by Google with the mission of producing a mass-market ubiquitous computer. Google Glass displays information in a smartphone-like hands-free format.




Google Glass syncs with your phone though bluetooth and allows you to receive your notifications directly on the screen above your right eye, instead of fetching your phone to respond. Glass is able to take pictures, video, answer calls, send text messages, post to Twitter, post to Facebook and many more things without the necessity of picking up your phone.



Contrary to popular belief, Google Glass cannot operate on its own and it cannot record video or take pictures without the crystal lighting up. If the crystal is off, the device isn't recording.

As of now, the only version available is the developer edition, and you can get it for $1500 here. A consumer edition is expected from Google in the foreseeable future.
